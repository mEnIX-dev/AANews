“Nuclear Monster” Linked to Secret Experiment at Hunterston

  HUNTERSTON, UNITED KINGDOM — A strange scientific report from the Neo-Genetics Institute claims that a mysterious creature known as Subject 99, nicknamed the “Nuclear Monster,” is real. According to leaked documents from a classified project called Project Chimera, the organism is the first successful hybrid created from the DNA of four very different species: a human, a giant panda, a Golden Eagle, and a Brazilian Wandering Spider.

The creature reportedly stands about 2.5 meters tall and weighs close to 300 kilograms. Its main body resembles that of a giant panda, covered with thick black-and-white fur that scientists say could act as natural armor, possibly even resisting bullets. However, its limbs are far from normal. Instead of human arms, Subject 99 has six spider-like legs with multiple joints. These limbs can produce extremely strong silk strands from the tips of its fingers.

Another remarkable feature is its wings. Inspired by the Golden Eagle, they stretch nearly five meters across and supposedly allow the heavy creature to fly at speeds reaching 120 km/h. Despite its unusual body, the face still looks partly human. Its eyes, however, are described as highly advanced, capable of detecting heat in every direction with a 360-degree field of vision.

Even more unusual is its diet. The report claims the creature feeds on bamboo and enriched uranium, using a “cold fusion stomach” to produce energy. Lead scientist Dr. Aris Totle says spider DNA helps it counter gravity through atomic vibrations at 440 Hz.

The experiments were allegedly conducted in hidden laboratories beneath the old reactors at the Hunterston nuclear site in Scotland.
